**Full voiceover:**
"A tumor or blood sample is a mixture. Flow cytometry helps separate that mixture conceptually, cell by cell. Fluorescent antibodies label specific proteins. Cells pass single file through lasers, and the machine records light signals for each cell. Those signals become plots where populations appear: T cells, tumor cells, activated cells, rare cells. Instead of averaging a whole sample, flow cytometry shows the mixture. That is why it matters so much in cancer research and immunology."
