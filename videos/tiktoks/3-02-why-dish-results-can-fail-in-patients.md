**Book:** Cancer Research  
**Chapters:** research methods bridge to 71-74

**Learning promise:** viewers understand model validity.

**Hook:** "The cancer cell in a dish is not the cancer in a person."

**Full voiceover:**
"A drug can look brilliant in a dish. But the cleaner the model, the more biology may be missing. A patient tumor has blood vessels, immune cells, matrix, oxygen gradients, drug delivery barriers, and evolutionary diversity. That is why researchers use a ladder of models: two-dimensional culture, spheroids, organoids, mouse models, and clinical trials. Each one trades control for realism. The point is not that simple models are useless. It is knowing what question each model can honestly answer."
