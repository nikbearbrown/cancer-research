## TikTok 3.1 — Diagnosis Starts with Seeing and Sampling

**Book:** Cancer Research  
**Chapters:** 28-30  
**Length target:** 65 seconds  
**Learning promise:** viewers understand why imaging and tissue sampling answer different questions.

**Hook:** "A scan can show where the mass is. It cannot fully tell you what it is."

**Shot-by-shot timeline:**

| Time | Visual | Voiceover | On-screen text |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 0-4 | CT/MRI-like scan points to a mass. | "A scan can show where the mass is." | Imaging: where? |
| 4-14 | Scan expands to show size and nearby anatomy. | "Imaging shows location, size, invasion, and possible spread." | Size / extent / anatomy |
| 14-24 | Biopsy needle icon enters. | "But it often cannot fully tell you what the cells are." | Tissue: what is it? |
| 24-36 | Pathology slide appears. | "A tissue sample lets pathology identify cancer type, grade, and markers." | Pathology |
| 36-49 | Molecular report appears. | "Molecular diagnostics can identify drivers and treatment-relevant biomarkers." | Molecular features |
| 49-65 | Layered stack: location, tissue, stage, biomarkers. | "Modern diagnosis is layered evidence, not one answer." | Diagnosis = layered model |

**Full voiceover:**
"A scan can show where the mass is. It can show location, size, invasion, and possible spread. But imaging often cannot fully tell you what the cells are. That is why tissue matters. A biopsy lets pathology identify cancer type, grade, and markers. Molecular diagnostics can identify drivers and treatment-relevant biomarkers. The modern diagnosis is a layered model: location, tissue identity, stage, molecular features, and treatment implications."

**Caption copy:** Imaging and tissue sampling are complementary. They answer different diagnostic questions.
