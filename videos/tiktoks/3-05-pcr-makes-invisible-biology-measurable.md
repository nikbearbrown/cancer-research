**Full voiceover:**
"PCR is a biological copy machine. It starts with a tiny DNA signal and copies one specific sequence again and again. After enough cycles, something too small to detect becomes measurable. qPCR adds timing: earlier signal means more starting material. With reverse transcription, RNA can be converted to cDNA, so gene expression can be measured too. In cancer, that can mean detecting mutations, checking markers, tracking response, or validating an experiment."
