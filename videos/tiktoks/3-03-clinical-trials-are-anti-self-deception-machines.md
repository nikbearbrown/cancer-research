**Book:** Cancer Research  
**Chapters:** 49 bridge  
**Length target:** 60 seconds  
**Learning promise:** viewers understand trial design as bias control.

**Hook:** "A clinical trial is how medicine tries not to fool itself."

**Shot-by-shot timeline:**

| Time | Visual | Voiceover | On-screen text |

**Full voiceover:**
"A clinical trial is how medicine tries not to fool itself. Cancer can fluctuate. Patients differ. Clinicians have expectations. Companies have incentives. Randomization helps balance differences between groups. Control groups show what would have happened without the new treatment. Endpoints matter: shrinking a tumor is not the same as helping someone live longer or live better. Blinding, predefined analysis, and statistical power reduce self-deception. A good trial asks a clean question and measures the outcome that matters."
